Welcome to George Mason University Department of Computer Science

The Computer Science graduate program is designed to equip students with the knowledge and skills necessary for both research and professional practice in the rapidly evolving field of computer science. The program offers a balanced curriculum that covers fundamental concepts as well as advanced topics in areas such as artificial intelligence, databases, programming languages, software engineering, systems and networks, theoretical computer science, and visual computing. Courses are structured into two categories: basic courses, which do not require prior graduate coursework, and advanced courses, which build on previous graduate-level classes. Most graduate classes are scheduled during the late afternoon and evening to accommodate working professionals.

In addition to a strong academic foundation, the program offers opportunities for financial assistance in the form of graduate assistantships, which may be available to full-time, degree-seeking students. These assistantships provide valuable teaching or research experience while helping to offset the cost of tuition. The program's flexibility and support make it an attractive option for students who are balancing their education with professional or personal commitments.

With computing becoming a vital tool in a wide array of disciplines, the Computer Science department emphasizes the principle of "Computing for All." The department strives to make computing accessible to students from diverse backgrounds by offering a broad range of undergraduate and graduate programs.
